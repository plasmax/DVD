I would like to plan a systematic, scientific experiment to identify the source of different artifacts that emerge during inference. Most of these occur when inferencing at values that don't match the window size, and resolution used during training.

Artifact Types
- Aspect Ratio
I've noticed that in video with wider aspect ratios, there's a fairly consistently sized artifact on either side of the images which looks like two vertical bars. My hypothesis is that this is due to training at a fixed resolution, and the solution would be to train at varying aspect ratios (which I believe the original [Wan 2.1](papers/Wan2.1_2503.20314v2.md) did, even if the [DVD LoRA](papers/DVD_2603.12250v1.md) did not). As a short term fix for inference, however, an interim solution could be to squash the image into a 4:3 aspect ratio while we retrain the model.

- Horizontal lines
These occur mostly in the lower half of the image, sometimes with a stripe across the middle. The lines can be vertical, too, but they are horizontal for the most part. My current leading hypothesis is that it could be due to training at a fixed resolution (640x480), but that might be only one of the causes. It's been suggested this looks like an interference pattern, and it also looks similar to some artifacting I've seen due to bfloat16 types. Since I switched to variable resolution, this has been much less present in predicted depth passes.

- Temporal Ghosting
This is the one that really needs testing and researching. I'm not 100% confident on my current hypothesis, which is that this is caused by using a fixed window size during training - switching to a variable window size hasn't reduced the problem as effectively as variable resolution did for the horizontal line artifacts. Whether these artifacts are due to the VAE, the temporal attention, or something else, I don't know. The artifacts present mostly towards the beginning of each inference window, and in most cases, reflect a detail that is present in the same area of the image that appears towards the end of the window - almost like some of the detail between the start and end is being shared somehow, but only in fragments. Ghosting shows up very visibly in window which change signficantly from the beginning to the end, and seems to be correllated with window size - it occurs a lot less at 45 frame windows, whereas in DVD's recommended 81 frame window, it's often present. I'd really like to devise a set of experiments to prove the source of the ghosting.

- Haloing
Possibly caused by the LMR loss function, there is often a soft dark edge separating objects from one another - almost like a drop shadow. It's not hugely noticeable when viewing in 2D, but does look a bit like the geometry is coming out incorrectly in those regions. This is a lower priority fix than the other issues.
