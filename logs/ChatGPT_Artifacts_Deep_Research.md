## Per-Artifact Deep Dive

**Aspect-ratio bars.** The literature on ViTs and diffusion transformers is very consistent on one point: native handling of arbitrary resolutions and aspect ratios has to be learned explicitly, otherwise performance degrades outside the training geometry. NaViT was proposed precisely because standard ViT practice of resizing to a fixed geometry is suboptimal, and FiT was proposed because ordinary DiTs struggle to generalize to unrestricted resolutions and aspect ratios. RoPE helps extrapolation relative to older embeddings, but even the dedicated RoPE-ViT study frames that as *improved* extrapolation, not immunity to large distribution shifts. In your system, the Wan DiT patchifies latent videos with no temporal patching, applies full attention across the entire token set, and uses axial 3D RoPE whose width component must now represent wider horizontal spans than in training. That makes the best explanation for left/right ŌĆ£barsŌĆØ a **horizontal-span OOD failure in the DiT**, not a generic VAE issue. Confidence: **high**. Secondary cause: the frozen decoder may sharpen the boundary of the bad region into a visible bar. Confidence: **medium**. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn35search0Ņłéturn35search2Ņłéturn34search0Ņłéturn34search2Ņłéturn13view0Ņłéturn12view0Ņłéturn15view1Ņłü

One subtle point matters here. The Wan VAE is mostly convolutional and therefore much less tied to a specific aspect ratio than the DiT. Its bottleneck attention still sees the spatio-temporal grid, but it does **not** use the same explicit RoPE machinery that the DiT uses. That makes the VAE a weaker primary suspect for bars that appear specifically when width grows beyond training experience. The released Wan code also allocates RoPE dimensions axially across time, height, and width; when width grows, attention must compare tokens over larger horizontal phase differences everywhere in the frame, which can degrade both edges rather than only the newly added far-right positions. That matches the ŌĆ£two consistent side regionsŌĆØ observation better than a pure decoder-padding story. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn13view0Ņłéturn12view0Ņłéturn4view1Ņłéturn4view5Ņłü

**Horizontal lines and interference patterns.** The official Wan VAE code contains an explicit fp32 cast around nearest-neighbor upsampling because bf16 interpolation was problematic enough to warrant a hard fix in the released model. That alone does **not** prove bf16 is your main culprit, but it is direct evidence that this stack has precision-sensitive image-lattice operations. At the same time, the classic deconvolution/checkerboard literature shows that multistage upsampling pipelines can create structured spatial artifacts when the sampling lattice and kernel overlap interact unfavorably. Because your line pattern became much smaller after switching from fixed to variable resolution training, the most likely explanation is a **learned resolution-specific lattice artifact**: the model had partially memorized position/frequency structure tied to one geometry, and the decoder/latent lattice made that structure visible. Confidence: **high**. A pure bf16 explanation would not normally improve this much just by broadening the training resolution distribution. Confidence: **medium-low**. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn4view2Ņłéturn33search0Ņłéturn33search4Ņłéturn34search0Ņłéturn35search0Ņłéturn35search2Ņłü

A useful way to think about the remaining faint lines is as a **beat pattern between three grids**: the VAEŌĆÖs 8├Ś spatial compression lattice, the DiTŌĆÖs 2├Ś2 latent patch lattice, and the output decoderŌĆÖs upsampling lattice. Fixed-resolution training makes those grids phase-locked to one image size. Variable-resolution training breaks that lock, so the artifact weakens but does not necessarily vanish. That is also why you sometimes see a dominant mid-image stripe or lower-half emphasis instead of a classic full-frame checkerboard: the artifact is likely being modulated by content, by the decoder receptive field, and by local attention/feature energy, not just by bare interpolation error. **Temporal ghosting.** This is the artifact where the architecture points most strongly in one direction. The Wan VAE is causal: its convolutions are left-padded in time and its attention uses block-causal masks. If the released code matches your frozen VAE, the VAE by itself should not provide a direct path for frame *t+k* to contaminate frame *t*. The released Wan transformer, however, is fully bidirectional over the entire spatio-temporal token set with no causal mask. So the first module in your pipeline that can actually move late-window evidence into early-window predictions is the **DiT self-attention**. That matches your observed symptom almost too well: fragments from the **end** of the window appear near the **start** of the window, and the effect gets worse when the window is longer and when scene content changes more within the window. The next layer of evidence is also consistent. Recent long-video diffusion work introduces segmented or localized attention because maintaining long-range coherence with unrestricted global attention is difficult, and one 2025 efficiency paper reports that attention in pretrained video diffusion models concentrates predominantly in localized 3D windows. Separately, work on long-context RoPE shows that extrapolation beyond training lengths can preserve some behavior but often introduces attention uncertainty and degraded long-range use. Your official DVD config trains on 45-frame windows by default; even with your local variable-window changes, 81-frame inference is still at or beyond the high end of what the model was taught to handle. Put simply: at long windows, the DiT has more distant frames it is *allowed* to use, less training prior on how to use them cleanly, and more incentive under deterministic regression to borrow information from the wrong time if that reduces latent MSE. There is also a strong architectural reason the artifact is biased toward the **beginning** of a window. Because the VAE is causal, early latent frames are ŌĆ£cold startŌĆØ frames: they have the least preceding temporal context and the most left-padding influence. That makes the early-frame representation the most ambiguous part of the encoded sequence. Once those ambiguous early latents enter a **non-causal** DiT, the easiest way for the network to reduce per-frame regression loss is to borrow from later, more informative frames, especially in slowly or smoothly changing scenes. When scene change becomes large, that shortcut becomes visually wrong and appears as late-content ghosts. **Haloing at depth edges.** DVDŌĆÖs own motivation for LMR is to fight deterministic-regression over-smoothing by enforcing differential constraints in latent space. That is directionally sensible, but finite-difference gradient matching does not enforce monotone step transitions at object boundaries; it only asks the predicted gradient field to resemble the target gradient field. At sharp depth discontinuities, that leaves room for one-pixel over- or undershoot, especially when combined with L2 regression pressure, latent compression, and a decoder that was trained for RGB video rather than for clean scalar depth discontinuities. The literature on monocular depth repeatedly highlights depth boundaries as the most failure-prone regions, and recent boundary-focused methods exist precisely because standard losses do not resolve those regions cleanly. For your ŌĆ£soft dark separationŌĆØ description, the most likely mechanism is a **small undershoot/ringing band at discontinuities** rather than global mis-scaling. Confidence: **medium**. ## VAE Analysis

The released Wan VAE is unusually important here because it is both **frozen** and **causal**. The code shows left-padded `CausalConv3d`, a temporal cache (`CACHE_T = 2`), block-causal attention in the bottleneck, and an upsample path with an explicit fp32 workaround for bf16 interpolation. The Wan 1.3B config reports `vae_stride = (4, 8, 8)`, so temporal compression is 4├Ś and spatial compression is 8├Ś. In practice that means a latent frame aggregates information over a short temporal neighborhood rather than corresponding to a single input frame. That aggregation is helpful for video generation, but it is a liability when you care about clean depth boundaries and precise frame-local geometry. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn17search2Ņłéturn4view0Ņłéturn4view1Ņłéturn4view2Ņłéturn4view5Ņłü

Two consequences follow. First, the **beginning of every independently encoded window is structurally weaker** than the middle or end, because causal left-padding substitutes missing past context with padding and cached warm-up. Second, the VAE can blur or entangle motion over its temporal stride even without violating causality. So the VAE is a credible amplifier of start-of-window instability, but not a credible primary source of the specific ŌĆ£future appears in the pastŌĆØ signature unless the released causal masking is broken in your local branch. That is why I view the VAE as a susceptibility source for ghosting, not its main generator. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn4view1Ņłéturn4view5Ņłéturn39search9Ņłü

On spatial artifacts, the VAE is much more plausible. Multistage upsampling is a classic source of structured artifacts, and the Wan code explicitly acknowledges one bf16 interpolation failure mode. Because your downstream target is depth packed into a latent/video format the VAE was never trained to decode faithfully, it is reasonable to expect subtle ringing, edge darkening, or banding even when the DiT latent is otherwise reasonable. The frozen VAE is therefore a strong suspect for **haloing** and a medium suspect for **residual line artifacts**, but only a weak suspect for true future-to-past temporal contamination. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn4view2Ņłéturn33search0Ņłéturn33search4Ņłü

At untrained resolutions and aspect ratios, the VAE should degrade more gracefully than the DiT because most of its machinery is convolutional. The weak point is its bottleneck attention and its multiscale down/up lattice, not absolute positional indexing. That asymmetry is diagnostically useful: if an artifact survives a VAE-only round trip and scales with aspect ratio, it is more likely decoder/lattice related; if it appears only after the DiT and grows sharply with width or window length, it is more likely transformer-related. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn4view1Ņłéturn4view5Ņłéturn13view0Ņłéturn35search2Ņłü

## Attention and Positional Encoding Analysis

The Wan DiT uses patch size `(1, 2, 2)`, hidden size 1536, 12 heads, 30 layers, and full self-attention with `window_size=(-1,-1)`. RoPE is applied axially over time, height, and width. The released code constructs per-head rotary dimensions so that time gets 44 real dimensions and height/width get 42 each for the 1.3B modelŌĆÖs 128-d head. The crucial point is not the exact split; it is that every token can attend everywhere, and the positional scheme is asked to organize a very large 3D token graph without any temporal causality or locality prior at inference. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn17search2Ņłéturn13view0Ņłéturn12view0Ņłéturn15view1Ņłü

That design is powerful in a generative backbone, but in deterministic depth regression it creates a different optimization pressure. Because your objective is a direct latent prediction loss rather than iterative denoising with stochastic rollouts, the model is free to use any temporally distant token if doing so reduces the instantaneous regression objective. In ambiguous regions, that encourages **feature borrowing across time**, which can look like mean-like temporal blending rather than random diffusion noise. This is why the DVD paperŌĆÖs ŌĆ£regression-induced over-smoothingŌĆØ discussion matters: LMR may sharpen local derivatives, but it does not stop the model from using the wrong frame as evidence. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn18view0Ņłéturn31view7Ņłéturn40search3Ņłü

The RoPE issue is real but should be framed correctly. The 1024 limit in the Wan code is a **per-axis frequency table size**, not the operative failure mode for your clips; your heights, widths, and latent frame counts are well below that. The relevant problem is **distributional extrapolation**, not table exhaustion. Long-context RoPE studies repeatedly find that behavior degrades beyond training lengths because attention patterns become unstable or uncertain, and broader transformer literature shows that longer contexts can produce strong positional bias and poor use of middle or distant content. Translating that to your case: when window length or horizontal span exceeds what the LoRA-adapted backbone saw often enough during training, temporal and width attention become less trustworthy exactly where your artifacts intensify. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn33search1Ņłéturn39search23Ņłéturn34search0Ņłéturn34search2Ņłü

For analysis tools, raw attention maps are useful but insufficient because information gets mixed across layers. The best-supported methods are to aggregate attention across layers with **attention rollout/attention flow** and to combine this with gradient-aware transformer attribution. For your case, I would not rely on visual heatmaps alone. I would compute a **temporal distance histogram of effective attention mass** and a **counterfactual influence matrix** measuring how much output frame *t* changes when input frame *s* is perturbed. If the ghosting story is correct, early outputs will show abnormally large sensitivity to late inputs in the affected windows. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn38search23Ņłéturn38search7Ņłéturn38search19Ņłü

## Experimental Design

**VAE round-trip baseline.** Hypothesis: some portion of the line and halo artifacts is introduced by the frozen VAE independent of the DiT. Independent variables: input type (RGB video, packed ground-truth depth video, synthetic step-edge depth charts), precision (bf16 vs fp32), and resolution/aspect ratio. Dependent variables: bar presence, line energy in the spatial Fourier spectrum, edge overshoot at depth steps, and start-of-window temporal PSNR/SSIM drift. If the hypothesis is correct, at least part of the bars/lines/halos will appear after encodeŌåÆdecode alone. If incorrect, the round trip will stay substantially cleaner than the full pipeline. Complexity: **low**. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn4view1Ņłéturn4view2Ņłéturn33search0Ņłü

**PrefixŌĆōsuffix counterfactual.** Hypothesis: temporal ghosting is caused by future-frame leakage in the DiT. Hold the first *N* frames of a window fixed and swap only the last *M* frames with a very different suffix; then compare predicted depth only on the fixed prefix. If early outputs change materially when only the suffix changes, the DiT is using future information. If the change is strongest in the earliest frames and grows with larger windows, that specifically supports the ŌĆ£DiT leakage + VAE warm-up susceptibilityŌĆØ story. If early outputs do not move, ghosting is more likely downstream. Complexity: **low** and, in my view, the single best experiment in the set. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn13view0Ņłéturn15view1Ņłéturn4view1Ņłéturn4view5Ņłü

**Attention leakage audit.** Hypothesis: affected checkpoints allocate too much effective attention mass from early queries to late keys. Log raw self-attention from a subset of layers and heads, then compute attention-flow/rollout and summarize mass by temporal offset. Expected result if correct: the ghosting checkpoints will show a heavier long-range tail, especially from early-frame queries, and the tail will grow between 45- and 81-frame windows. If incorrect, attention will remain mostly local and the cause is more likely VAE/decoder. Complexity: **medium**, because instrumenting a 30-layer video DiT is tedious but straightforward. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn38search23Ņłéturn38search7Ņłéturn40search3Ņłü

**Resolution and aspect-ratio sweep.** Hypothesis: aspect-ratio bars and residual lines are width/resolution generalization failures rather than content-specific artifacts. Use the same clips, keep the latent-frame count fixed, and sweep width while holding height constant, then sweep overall scale while preserving aspect ratio. Measure bar width in pixels, bar energy at the image margins, and line energy in the 2D FFT. If correct, bars should grow systematically with horizontal span and lines should peak at particular lattice-aligned resolutions. If incorrect, the artifact will be mostly content-driven or numerically unstable with no clear geometry trend. Complexity: **low**. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn35search0Ņłéturn35search2Ņłéturn34search0Ņłéturn34search2Ņłü

**Precision sweep and decoder isolation.** Hypothesis: bf16 is a secondary contributor to residual striping. Run the same checkpoint in fp32 through the VAE only, then through the full pipeline, and compare with bf16. If only VAE round-trip artifacts improve, the sensitivity is mostly in decoder interpolation or convolution accumulation. If only the full pipeline improves, DiT math or attention softmax is participating. If nothing changes, the artifact is learned rather than numerical. Complexity: **low**. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn4view2Ņłéturn11view1Ņłéturn11view2Ņłü

**Temporal locality ablation.** Hypothesis: restricting or biasing attention to local temporal neighborhoods will reduce ghosting more than it harms depth quality. The cleanest version is to impose a temporal attention mask or radius at inference or to zero out far-temporal attention weights in selected layers. Long-video diffusion work and sliding-tile attention both suggest that useful attention in pretrained video models is predominantly local anyway. If correct, ghosting should drop sharply before static depth quality drops much. If incorrect, the model truly depends on long-range temporal evidence. Complexity: **high** if you need to modify the released attention kernels, but the expected payoff is large. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn40search3Ņłéturn38search20Ņłü

**Loss-side ablation for haloing.** Hypothesis: haloing comes from the interaction of latent L2 regression and finite-difference LMR. Train short controlled runs with L2-only, L2+LMR, and L2+an edge-aware boundary loss operating after VAE decode or on decoded depth edges. If the current explanation is correct, L2-only will blur boundaries, L2+LMR will sharpen them but retain undershoot halos, and an explicitly edge-aware monotonic or boundary loss should reduce the dark separation band. If all three retain the same edge shadow, the decoder is a stronger suspect. Complexity: **medium**. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn18view0Ņłéturn36search8Ņłéturn36search20Ņłéturn37search7Ņłü
