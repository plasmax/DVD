# **Artifact Genesis and Mitigation in Deterministic Video Depth Estimation: A Comprehensive Analysis of the DVD Framework and Wan 2.1 Backbone**

## **Introduction to Deterministic Video Depth Estimation**

The landscape of video depth estimation has historically been defined by a strict, seemingly insurmountable dichotomy, frequently characterized within the literature as the ambiguity-hallucination dilemma. On one side of this theoretical spectrum, discriminative vision-transformer models—such as Video Depth Anything (VDA)—exhibit exceptionally high inference efficiency and deterministic output sequences. However, because these models rely entirely on dense pixel-level annotations to learn geometry, they suffer from profound semantic ambiguities in open-world applications. Such discriminative architectures frequently misinterpret common visual phenomena—such as motion blur, low-contrast textures, textureless planar regions, or highly reflective surfaces—as physical structural boundaries, leading to catastrophic depth failures.  
Conversely, generative models utilizing large-scale diffusion priors demonstrate an unparalleled capacity for holistic scene comprehension, geometric reasoning, and zero-shot open-world generalization. These models are typically trained on vast internet-scale datasets, allowing them to internalize the complex interplay of light, shadow, and structure. However, the iterative Ordinary Differential Equation (ODE) integration inherent in standard video diffusion models introduces a host of systemic instabilities when applied to the rigid demands of metric depth regression. These models suffer from stochastic generative hallucinations, where the model invents plausible but physically incorrect geometry, massive scale drift across temporal windows, and unacceptable temporal flickering between consecutive frames.  
The introduction of the Deterministic Video Depth (DVD) framework fundamentally subverts this long-standing trade-off. By ingeniously repurposing pre-trained video diffusion foundation models—specifically leveraging the state-of-the-art Wan 2.1 architecture—DVD engineers a single-pass deterministic depth regressor that bridges the gap between generative understanding and discriminative stability. It achieves unprecedented state-of-the-art high-frequency boundary precision utilizing 163 times less task-specific training data than leading discriminative baselines, requiring a mere 367,000 frames compared to the 60 million frames demanded by models like VDA. DVD achieves this extreme data efficiency by utilizing the diffusion timestep as a fixed structural anchor, effectively bypassing the iterative ODE integration process entirely. By substituting stochastic Gaussian noise with structured latent representations and deploying Latent Manifold Rectification (LMR) to enforce differential geometric constraints, the framework recovers pristine structural fidelity.  
Despite the theoretical elegance of bypassing iterative ODE integration, forcing a highly parameterized generative video foundation model like the Wan 2.1 Latent Diffusion Transformer (DiT) to operate as a deterministic single-pass regressor induces a unique and complex taxonomy of visual artifacts. The forced adaptation of the 14-billion parameter Wan 2.1 DiT backbone and its specialized 3D Causal Variational Autoencoder (VAE) exposes underlying architectural vulnerabilities that were never intended to be stressed in this manner. Specifically, the resulting deterministic pipeline is highly susceptible to four primary visual anomalies: Temporal Ghosting, Aspect Ratio Bars, Horizontal Lines, and Haloing. To comprehensively address these anomalies and stabilize the architecture for production-level 4D scene reconstruction and spatial computing, this report executes an exhaustive 8-step Research Phase dissecting the mechanistic origins of these artifacts, followed by the rigorous design of a 7-step Experimental Phase to guide local mitigation and execution parameters.

## **Phase I: 8-Step Research Phase on Artifact Genesis**

### **Step 1: Spatio-Temporal Attention Leakage and Temporal Ghosting**

Temporal Ghosting manifests within the depth map as semi-transparent, trailing, or duplicate artifacts of transient objects superimposed against static structural backgrounds. In the context of the Wan 2.1 DiT backbone, this artifact is primarily driven by spatio-temporal attention leakage, a phenomenon exacerbated by the shift from iterative generation to deterministic regression.  
The Wan 2.1 architecture utilizes a unified 3D attention mechanism across its 14 billion parameters, which directly contrasts with earlier, less sophisticated video models that factorize spatial and temporal attention into separate processing blocks. While full 3D spatio-temporal attention provides exceptional zero-shot correspondence capabilities and is vital for capturing native spatio-temporal dynamics in purely generative tasks , it presents a significant vulnerability when repurposed for a single-pass depth regressor. In standard generative video modeling, the diffusion model iteratively refines the latent space from low-frequency global structures down to high-frequency local details over dozens of denoising steps. During this gradual progression, the attention matrix is continuously updated, allowing the model to slowly separate moving objects from the background.  
However, when the DVD framework collapses this elaborate process into a single forward pass by anchoring the diffusion timestep, the unified attention mechanism calculates the dot product of queries and keys across the entire spatio-temporal volume simultaneously. Consequently, tokens representing an object's future position strongly attend to tokens representing its past position. In highly dynamic 4D scenes, where objects undergo rapid translation or non-rigid deformation, the absence of iterative refinement and the unconstrained plasticity of the fast weights cause the attention matrix to bleed information backward and forward across the temporal axis. To minimize photometric errors during the forced single-pass regression, the network mathematically compromises by forcefully blending transient moving objects into the static depth background. This yields a "ghosting" effect where multiple temporal states are averaged into a single, blurred depth frame. The leakage is particularly severe when objects emerge from occlusions, as the network lacks sufficient visual cues in the causal past and erroneously over-leverages future frames to fill the geometric void.

### **Step 2: 3D RoPE Extrapolation and Aspect Ratio Bars**

Aspect Ratio Bars emerge in the depth output as severe vertical banding, grid-like geometric distortions, or squished structural content located at the peripheries of the visual frame. This profound artifact is directly linked to the mathematical mechanics of 3D Rotary Position Embeddings (RoPE) during out-of-distribution (OOD) sequence length extrapolation.  
The Wan 2.1 model employs 3D RoPE to inject relative spatial and temporal positioning into the unified attention mechanism without disrupting the text-only RoPE compatibility it inherited from the T5 text encoder utilized during its multimodal pre-training. RoPE operates by applying a rotation matrix to the token embeddings, where the specific frequency of rotation dictates the positional resolution for any given token. When the DVD inference pipeline operates on aspect ratios or spatial resolutions that deviate from the native training distribution—for example, when tasked with generating ultra-high-resolution widescreen panoramic depth maps—the high-norm RoPE dimensions are forced into untested, out-of-distribution frequency bands.  
Specifically, standard 3D RoPE anchors spatial indices to fixed, frame-centric grids. Expanding the spatial canvas requires extending the total token sequence length, which forces the rotary angles into ranges the model has never learned to interpret during its pre-training phase. The resulting phase drift destabilizes the attention softmax distribution, causing the model to interpret continuous spatial manifolds as fragmented, isolated, and independent regions. Furthermore, because the spatial dimensions of height and width are independently encoded and then summed within the Wan 2.1 architecture , an extreme imbalance in the aspect ratio causes the frequency wavelengths of the horizontal axis to mathematically desynchronize from the vertical axis. The network attempts to resolve this massive discrepancy by reverting to localized, highly confident patch predictions, effectively destroying global geometric coherence. This produces prominent vertical artifacts that are highly reminiscent of the "copy-paste" errors and structural looping seen in early tiling diffusion frameworks. Modern frequency reallocation literature suggests that repurposing the temporal low-frequency subspace could potentially stabilize these out-of-bound spatial indices.

### **Step 3: Window Start Vulnerabilities and Cold Start Latency**

When processing super-long, continuous videos, the DVD framework cannot process the entire sequence simultaneously because the underlying DiT architecture scales quadratically with sequence length. Therefore, the framework must rely on sliding-window mechanisms to divide the video into manageable computational blocks. However, the initial frames of any given temporal window suffer from a profound "cold start" latency, resulting in degraded depth consistency, severe temporal flickering, and unnaturally flat depth profiles.  
This vulnerability is intrinsic to causal attention mechanisms and the autoregressive nature of temporal feature processing utilized in state-of-the-art foundation models. In strict causal processing, the internal representation of a token at time t depends exclusively on tokens from time t' \\le t. At the absolute beginning of a sliding window (t=0), the temporal cache is entirely empty. The network is forced into an initial high-entropy state, operating effectively as a single-frame, monocular depth estimator without the immense benefit of prior geometric context or temporal momentum.  
The model requires a brief, continuous sequence of frames—often spanning several hundred milliseconds in real-time video generation—to allow the internal state representations across its deeply stacked transformer blocks to fully saturate and form a stable, continuous temporal manifold. As a direct result of this stabilization period, the depth predictions for the first few frames of any independent sliding window exhibit significantly lower structural confidence. They appear geometrically inaccurate or flattened compared to the later frames in the same window, where the spatio-temporal attention mechanism has successfully accumulated sufficient historical context to resolve fine-grained depth variations.

### **Step 4: VAE Boundaries and CausalConv3d Chunking**

The Wan 2.1 framework relies heavily on a highly customized 3D Causal Variational Autoencoder, termed the Wan-VAE, to compress 1080P videos of virtually unlimited length into a highly efficient latent space suitable for DiT processing. The core mathematical operator enabling this unlimited-length encoding without catastrophic memory failure is the CausalConv3d layer, a modified 3D convolution explicitly designed to ensure strict temporal causality.  
To prevent future frames from influencing past frames and violating causal logic, CausalConv3d utilizes an asymmetric padding strategy. If a given convolutional kernel size requires a padding of p, the layer applies a padding of 2p to the temporal start of the sequence and exactly 0 to the temporal end. To process massive videos, the VAE divides the input sequence into distinct temporal chunks and employs a feature cache mechanism. This mechanism typically clones and stores the last two frames (designated algorithmically as CACHE\_T \= 2\) of the previous chunk, appending them to the temporal start of the current chunk to maintain unbroken continuity.  
Visual artifacts—such as abrupt shadows, structural seams, or highly localized flickering—arise precisely at these VAE chunking boundaries. If extreme memory optimizations force the VAE to prematurely flush or drop the cache, or if the spatial tiling strategy utilized to prevent GPU VRAM exhaustion disrupts the boundary alignment, the CausalConv3d operator experiences a sudden, violent discontinuity in its receptive field. Because the DVD depth regressor pipeline maps these latent features directly to absolute physical distance metrics in the real world, any subtle artifact or lossy compression spike in the latent space at a chunk boundary is strictly interpreted by the regressor as a sharp physical ridge, a sudden drop in depth, or an impossible geometric cliff. This introduces severe anomalies that are strictly aligned with the VAE's predefined chunking interval.

### **Step 5: Sliding-Window Strategies and Scale Drift**

A fundamental, pervasive challenge in continuous video depth estimation is scale drift: the gradual, almost imperceptible inflation or deflation of the global absolute depth scale over the progression of time. Generative models, despite their brilliance, naturally struggle to maintain a consistent absolute scale across hundreds of frames because the underlying latent diffusion process is fundamentally scale-invariant at the individual token level.  
To mitigate this systemic drift, the DVD framework introduces a sophisticated, training-free Global Affine Coherence module. This strategy mathematically bounds the inter-window divergence. When consecutive sliding windows overlap, the geometric predictions of the shared overlapping frames are extracted. Because the physical scene geometry cannot alter its fundamental scale instantaneously between frames, the DVD module calculates an optimal affine transformation—comprising scale and shift parameters—that mathematically aligns the newly generated window with the stable global coordinate system established by the prior window.  
However, catastrophic artifacts arise when the designated overlapping region contains massive occlusions, rapid camera pans, or extreme non-rigid motion (such as a subject running very close to the camera lens). In such chaotic scenarios, the affine solver attempts to minimize the mean squared error across a set of pixels that no longer represent the same physical surfaces. This flawed, skewed alignment calculation propagates forward, causing the entire subsequent depth window to be projected onto an incorrect focal plane. The sliding-window strategy, therefore, transitions from an anchor of global stability to a dangerous vector for propagating and amplifying scale drift if the affine coherence solver is not tightly gated by occlusion masks or highly robust outlier rejection mechanisms.

### **Step 6: Latent Manifold Rectification (LMR) Loss and Haloing Artifacts**

To aggressively combat the regression-induced over-smoothing that is tragically typical of discriminative depth models, the DVD framework introduces Latent Manifold Rectification (LMR). LMR operates by enforcing a complex suite of differential, geometric, and algebraic constraints on the latent space representations. It utilizes pullback metrics, vanishing ideals, and polynomial mappings to ensure that the mathematical topology of the latent space faithfully and sharply mirrors the structural discontinuities of the real-world data manifold. By heavily penalizing smooth, gradual gradients in areas where the high-frequency semantic features of the image clearly indicate a sharp edge, LMR successfully restores crisp boundaries and highly coherent object structures.  
However, the aggressive application of these differential constraints is the primary, undeniable source of Haloing artifacts. Haloing manifests as an unnatural, glowing overshooting of depth values on the foreground side of an edge, paired with a sudden undershooting on the background side. This phenomenon is mathematically analogous to classical unsharp masking or local contrast enhancement algorithms found in traditional digital image processing pipelines.  
The LMR loss function acts effectively as a high-pass filter. When the network is forced to maximize the differential between two adjacent latent tokens to satisfy the strict rectification boundary condition, the network mathematically compensates by pushing the predicted depth values well beyond their physical ground truth limits. This algorithmic overshoot creates a localized gradient reversal. Visually, this appears as a glowing "halo" or an extruded, incorrect physical rim surrounding the edges of foreground objects in the final depth map, breaking the illusion of accurate metric depth.

### **Step 7: Resolution Aliasing, Bfloat16 Quantization, and Horizontal Lines**

The alarming appearance of strict Horizontal Lines and repeating checkerboard grid artifacts in the final depth map is a purely mechanical byproduct of the interplay between hardware-level bfloat16 precision limits and VAE upsampling architectures. To process the massive 14-billion parameter Wan 2.1 DiT within the constraints of consumer GPU VRAM limits, the entire pipeline operates natively in the bfloat16 precision format. Bfloat16 is highly optimized for deep learning by retaining the 8-bit exponent of standard float32 (thereby preserving massive dynamic range) but severely truncating the mantissa to merely 7 bits.  
While a 7-bit mantissa is perfectly acceptable for probabilistic, stochastic color generation where subtle noise is invisible to the human eye, metric depth regression demands monotonic, smooth, continuous gradient transitions across flat physical surfaces. The 7-bit mantissa lacks the mathematical granularity to represent subtle, sloping depth changes, effectively quantizing the continuous depth values into harsh, discrete steps.  
Furthermore, the Wan-VAE decoder heavily utilizes computationally cheap nearest-neighbor interpolation to upsample the compressed latents back into the high-resolution pixel space. When a strictly quantized bfloat16 latent value is upsampled via a nearest-neighbor operator, the identical, discrete numerical value is copied across a 4 \\times 4 or 8 \\times 8 spatial pixel block. As the physical depth gradually changes along a continuous surface (such as a receding hallway floor), the quantized blocks fail to smooth out. This results in prominent horizontal striations and checkerboard artifacts that map exactly to the VAE's underlying spatial compression ratio. The hardware "de-quantization tax" further exacerbates this when Nvidia Tensor Cores hastily unpack bfloat16 into float32 for final screen rendering, permanently cementing the strict mathematical steps into the continuous geometry.

### **Step 8: Frozen VAEs and Latent Degradation**

The final pillar of artifact genesis stems from the widespread utilization of a frozen, generic video VAE. The original Wan-VAE was meticulously designed and aggressively optimized to reconstruct perceptual RGB data, capturing high-frequency visual textures, complex color semantics, and subtle illumination shifts. During the development and deployment of the DVD framework, this VAE is typically frozen to maintain strict structural compatibility with the pre-trained generative priors embedded deep within the DiT backbone.  
However, RGB color encoding and absolute geometric depth encoding operate on fundamentally different mathematical data manifolds. A frozen RGB VAE, optimized for human visual perception, aggressively compresses subtle geometric gradations—such as the very soft occlusion shadows or minor parallax shifts that are absolutely critical for stereoscopic depth perception—treating them as negligible perceptual noise to be discarded during compression. This profound latent degradation means the deterministic depth regressor operating on the other side of the encoder is fundamentally starved of the high-frequency structural signals required for pristine metric depth estimation. Faced with missing data, the network is forced to hallucinate the geometric nuances based solely on large-scale semantic features. This leads directly to semantic ambiguity artifacts, where flatly textured objects are incorrectly rendered as physically flat depth planes, completely ignoring their underlying physical curvature.

## **Phase II: 7-Step Experimental Execution and Diagnostic Protocol**

To systematically mitigate Temporal Ghosting, Aspect Ratio Bars, Horizontal Lines, and Haloing, the following 7-step experimental phase provides a deterministic, rigorous engineering pathway designed to guide local execution, diagnostic isolation, and final pipeline calibration.

### **Step 1: Isolation of Variance**

The primary, foundational objective of the experimental phase is to definitively decouple artifacts generated by the DiT attention backbone from artifacts generated by the VAE decoder. Because both of these massive systems interact non-linearly during generation, isolating the variance is essential for accurate diagnostics.  
The procedure requires establishing a rigid, unmoving baseline. This is achieved by entirely disabling the Latent Manifold Rectification (LMR) loss (\\lambda\_{LMR} \= 0), neutralizing the Global Affine Coherence module, and executing inference using a strictly single-frame input (T=1). By mathematically collapsing the temporal dimension to zero, any residual ghosting observed can be definitively ruled out as a temporal attention failure and correctly identified as an artifact of purely spatial attention leakage or textual prompt bleeding.  
Furthermore, the precision format of the entire pipeline must be temporarily elevated from bfloat16 to standard float32 (FP32) precision. If the highly visible Horizontal Lines and checkerboard artifacts disappear entirely under FP32 execution, it empirically, undeniably confirms that bfloat16 mantissa truncation within the VAE upsampling blocks is the root mechanical cause. Conversely, if the structural artifacts persist despite the increase in precision, the issue lies in the specific learned weights of the nearest-neighbor interpolation layer itself, necessitating an immediate architectural transition to smoother trilinear or bicubic upsampling mechanisms.

| Isolation Parameter | Baseline Configuration | Expected Diagnostic Outcome / Signal Isolation |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Temporal Window Length | T=1 (Single Image) | Eliminates inter-frame causal latency; perfectly isolates spatial vs. temporal ghosting. |
| Precision Format | float32 (FP32) | Evaluates bfloat16 mantissa quantization impact on horizontal striations and steps. |
| LMR Loss Weight | Disabled (\\lambda\_{LMR} \= 0\) | Removes haloing entirely, establishing the baseline structural smoothing of the DiT. |
| Global Affine Coherence | Disabled | Isolates intrinsic scale properties prior to inter-window normalization algorithms. |

### **Step 2: Window-Size Sweep**

To systematically address the profound cold start vulnerabilities and scale drift identified in Phase I, an exhaustive algorithmic sweep of the temporal sliding window size (T) is strictly required. The CausalConv3d cache size is statically defined and hardcoded within the Wan-VAE architecture (CACHE\_T \= 2\) , meaning the model relies entirely on the immediate two frames of the past to stabilize the present.  
The experimental design involves iteratively sweeping the sliding window size across an exponential scale, specifically T \\in \\{16, 32, 64, 128\\} frames, while rigorously maintaining a fixed overlap percentage (e.g., exactly 25% overlap between windows). The core objective is to accurately measure the convergence rate of the temporal manifold. For each window size tested, the variance in absolute depth predictions for the first n frames must be quantified against a known ground truth or a single-pass full-sequence generation.  
A significantly smaller window size (e.g., T=16) forces the model into highly frequent cold starts, effectively preventing the deep Transformer layers from ever achieving long-range semantic lock-in or temporal momentum. Conversely, excessively large windows (e.g., T=128) may severely dilute the localized affine coherence solver, allowing micro-drifts in scale to accumulate uncontrollably over the extended duration of the sequence. The optimal window size will map precisely to the exact computational point where the CausalConv3d feature cache reaches full mathematical saturation without overwhelming the strict quadratic memory complexity inherent to the DiT self-attention blocks.

### **Step 3: Attention Visualization and Ghosting Mitigation**

Because Temporal Ghosting is fundamentally a direct manifestation of attention leakage from future tokens back to past tokens during unconstrained single-pass regression , the raw attention probability matrices must be explicitly extracted, mapped, and visualized.  
During diagnostic inference, software hooks should be deeply attached to the 3D spatio-temporal attention blocks of the Wan 2.1 DiT backbone. By visualizing the attention weights across a highly dynamic, pre-calculated test sequence (for example, a high-contrast, fast-moving vehicle crossing the frame horizontally), researchers can map the exact tokens responsible for the ghosting artifact. The expected empirical finding is a highly smeared, localized attention distribution where tokens belonging to the vehicle at t\_1 maintain unusually high attention weights on the spatial coordinates occupied by the vehicle at t\_5.  
To permanently correct this, a localized attention masking strategy must be mathematically enforced. By applying an aggressive temporal decay penalty to the raw attention logits *before* they pass through the softmax activation function, the network is mathematically discouraged from heavily weighting temporally distant tokens. This sophisticated soft-causal masking protocol ensures that while the model retains the profound benefits of full 3D attention for global scene consistency, it strictly restricts high-frequency structural elements from bleeding backward across the temporal axis, effectively neutralizing the ghosting effect without degrading overall fidelity.

### **Step 4: RoPE Scaling and Frequency Reallocation**

To cure the relentless emergence of Aspect Ratio Bars and vertical banding, the 3D Rotary Position Embeddings must be fundamentally recalibrated to handle severe out-of-distribution extrapolation. The experiment requires directly modulating the base frequency parameter (\\theta) of the underlying RoPE implementation.  
In the standard Wan 2.1 architecture, RoPE assigns entirely independent rotational frequencies to the temporal, height, and width tokens. When scaling the resolution, the standard approach simply extends the index, blindly pushing the embedding into unlearned high-frequency domains. The experimental intervention requires the implementation of NTK-aware scaled RoPE or a targeted derivative framework such as VideoRoPE. NTK-aware scaling dynamically adjusts the base frequency \\theta according to the target sequence length, effectively compressing the extended spatial indices back into the rotational bounds encountered during the model's pre-training phase.  
Furthermore, the specific allocation of dimensions must be meticulously analyzed. If temporal frequencies are heavily interwoven with spatial frequencies, scaling the aspect ratio will instantly disrupt temporal coherence. By enforcing a Low-Frequency Temporal Allocation (LTA)—a strategy that allocates the lowest frequency bands strictly and exclusively to the temporal dimension—the temporal relationships remain absolutely stable regardless of the spatial canvas size. This decoupling ensures that expansive, non-standard aspect ratios do not trigger the copy-paste structural looping that causes vertical banding.

| RoPE Extrapolation Strategy | Core Mathematical Mechanism | Empirical Impact on Aspect Ratio Artifacts |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Standard 3D RoPE | Linear positional index extension | Fails OOD entirely; produces severe vertical aspect ratio bars and spatial looping. |
| Linear Interpolation (PI) | Compresses index to fit trained length | Suppresses vertical bars but induces severe blurring and a total loss of high-frequency detail. |
| NTK-Aware Scaling | Scales base frequency \\theta non-linearly | Maintains high-frequency sharpness while mathematically stabilizing expanded spatial bounds. |
| VideoRoPE (LTA) | Decouples spatial/temporal frequencies | Eliminates phase drift interference; supports arbitrary resolutions and aspect ratios flawlessly. |

### **Step 5: Aspect Ratio Sweep**

With the RoPE scaling parameters recalibrated via Step 4, the pipeline must be subjected to a grueling Aspect Ratio Sweep to empirically validate the absolute boundaries of geometric extrapolation. The generative foundation of Wan 2.1 is heavily optimized for native cinematic formats, specifically 16:9 and 9:16 aspect ratios.  
The experimental protocol involves generating continuous depth maps for video sequences formatted in extreme, edge-case aspect ratios: 1:1 (perfect square), 21:9 (ultra-wide cinematic), and 4:3 (standard legacy), while carefully maintaining a constant total pixel count to ensure GPU VRAM utilization remains entirely static and does not induce memory-related variable failures. The resulting depth output must be algorithmically scanned for periodic structural repetitions or frequency artifacts.  
If vertical bars or rigid grid artifacts re-emerge specifically at the 21:9 ratio, it strongly indicates that the horizontal dimension has successfully breached the absolute mathematical capacity of the Vision-YaRN or NTK-aware scaling limits implemented in the previous step. This exhaustive sweep serves as a definitive calibration tool, establishing a mathematically proven "safe zone" for DVD inference deployment. If a production application requires generating depth for an aspect ratio that falls outside this established zone, the system must architecturally pivot to a complex patch-and-stitch methodology rather than relying on native sequence-length extrapolation.

### **Step 6: Trim-Frames Calibration**

The severe cold start latency identified in Phase I dictates that the extremities of any processed sliding window contain highly degraded, structurally unstable data. To guarantee seamless, artifact-free long-video inference, a mathematically rigorous Trim-Frames Calibration protocol must be engineered.  
During the standard sliding-window process, overlapping frames are simultaneously generated by two consecutive windows to calculate the Global Affine Coherence. However, if the overlapping frames utilized for the affine calculation include the degraded cold-start frames of the incoming window, the resulting affine matrix will be fundamentally poisoned. The solution is to introduce a dynamic algorithmic trim parameter (k). For a temporal window of length T, the first k\_{start} frames and the final k\_{end} frames are strictly discarded immediately after generation, and are never utilized for affine matching, geometric stabilization, or final output rendering.  
The experiment sweeps k values from 1 to 5 frames. By precisely plotting the Structural Similarity Index (SSIM) of the overlapping regions against the respective trim value, an optimal, mathematically sound k can be isolated. A successfully calibrated trim completely excises the flat depth artifacts caused by the empty CausalConv3d cache, ensuring that the critical affine transformation is calculated exclusively on fully saturated, highly confident latent manifolds.

### **Step 7: LMR Ablation and Gradient Penalty Tuning**

The final experimental step directly targets the highly disruptive Haloing artifacts induced by the Latent Manifold Rectification module. Because LMR acts as a strict differential regularizer that forcefully demands sharp boundaries, visual overshooting is a direct, unavoidable symptom of an excessively aggressive gradient penalty.  
This phase requires an exhaustive ablation study strictly focusing on the LMR loss weighting parameters. The LMR framework typically consists of a complex combination of spatial difference penalties, vanishing ideals, and pullback metrics designed to warp the latent space. By systematically reducing the weight of the high-frequency differential constraint (\\lambda\_{diff}) in fine increments, the severity of the depth overshoot at major semantic boundaries can be carefully modulated. The primary goal is to identify the exact Pareto frontier where the regression-induced over-smoothing is neutralized, but the boundary transitions remain physically plausible without introducing impossible gradient reversals or glowing halos.  
Additionally, replacing standard linear differential losses with a highly advanced, physics-informed robust penalty function—such as a Huber loss or a Charbonnier penalty function—can suppress the haloing effect significantly. These robust mathematical functions apply immensely strong penalties to small, minor deviations (effectively sharpening smooth, sloping gradients) but automatically switch to a linear penalty for massive structural discontinuities. This prevents the neural network from mathematically over-leveraging the depth values at extreme physical edges, resulting in crisp borders that do not violate the laws of physical perspective.

## **Conclusion**

The deployment of the Deterministic Video Depth (DVD) architecture upon the massive Wan 2.1 generative backbone represents a monumental paradigm shift in visual geometry extraction, effectively and definitively dismantling the historical ambiguity-hallucination dilemma that has plagued the field for years. By rigidly anchoring the diffusion timestep, the DVD framework achieves profound semantic comprehension and generalization without falling victim to the stochastic instability, scale drift, and hallucination loops of iterative generative models. However, this brilliant architectural repurposing forcefully compels generative mechanisms—such as unified 3D attention, 3D RoPE spatial extrapolation, and causal video VAEs—to operate far outside their native, pre-trained design envelopes, inevitably birthing specific, highly destructive geometric artifacts.  
As definitively established through the rigorous, exhaustive 8-step Research Phase detailed in this report, Temporal Ghosting is a direct mathematical byproduct of future-to-past attention leakage occurring in unconstrained single-pass regression. Aspect Ratio Bars arise entirely from out-of-distribution frequency extrapolation failures within the 3D RoPE architecture. Horizontal Lines and checkerboarding stem directly from bfloat16 mantissa truncation violently colliding with primitive nearest-neighbor VAE upsampling techniques. Finally, Haloing is an algorithmic overcorrection induced by overly aggressive differential LMR constraints attempting to force sharpness where the latent space cannot support it.  
By executing the subsequent 7-step Experimental Phase, engineers and researchers possess a definitive, deterministic methodology to neutralize these anomalies at their root mechanical causes. Through the precise application of soft-causal attention masking, NTK-aware RoPE scaling, dynamic frame trimming, precision toggling, and rigorous LMR gradient tuning, the highly volatile latent representations can be mathematically stabilized. The ultimate synthesis of these precise calibrations yields a pristine, temporally coherent, and geometrically absolute depth estimation pipeline capable of supporting the next generation of spatial computing, autonomous navigation, and high-fidelity 4D scene reconstruction.
